About FUTA

The Federal University of Technology Akure (FUTA) is a leading institution of higher learning in Nigeria, established in 1981. The university is committed to providing world-class education in technology, science, and engineering disciplines.

FUTA offers a wide range of undergraduate and postgraduate programs across various schools including Engineering, Sciences, Agriculture, Environmental Technology, and more. The university is known for its excellent academic standards, research output, and state-of-the-art facilities.

As a prospective student, this portal will help you calculate your screening score and determine which courses you may be eligible for based on your academic performance.

Admission Process

The admission process at FUTA involves several steps:

1

Meet O'level requirements (5 credits including English and Mathematics)

2

Score above the UTME cut-off mark

3

Participate in the Post-UTME screening exercise

4

Meet the departmental cut-off marks

How to Calculate Your Screening Result

The FUTA screening score is calculated using your O'level results and JAMB score, using 2025 calculation of 75:25:

O'level Calculation (25 points maximum)

Only 20 points can be attained from grades; remaining 5 points are decided by school/catchment

A1 = 80 points
B2 = 72 points
B3 = 67 points
C4 = 62 points
C5 = 57 points
C6 = 52 points

Formula: Add points for 5 subjects → Divide by 5 → Divide by 100 → Multiply by 25

JAMB Calculation (75 points maximum)

Formula: (JAMB Score ÷ 400) × 75

Total Aggregate

Total = O'level Points + JAMB Points

FUTA Departmental Cut-off Marks

The School of Electrical Systems Engineering (SESE) and School of Infrastructure Minerals and Manufacturing Engineering (SIMME) are formerly the School of Engineering and Engineering Technology (SEET).

The College of Health Sciences (CHS) consists of School of Basic Medical Science (SBMS), School of Basic Clinical Science (SBCS) and School of Clinical Science (SCS).

The School of Logistics and Innovation Technology (SLIT) was formerly called the School of Management Technology (SMAT).
